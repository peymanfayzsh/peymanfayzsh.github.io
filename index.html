About me

I am a PhD researcher at the Research Centre in Intelligent Technologies (CiTIUS), University of Santiago de Compostela, Spain, funded by the European Union’s Horizon 2020 Marie Skłodowska-Curie project MENELAOSNT. From February 2021 to August 2021, I had the privilege of serving as a Visiting Researcher at pmdtechnologies in Siegen, Germany, where I explored pixel design for indirect Time-of-Flight (iToF) image sensors. My research focuses on the design and characterization of CMOS image sensors, computational sensing, and micro/nanofabrication. I am passionate about advancing mixed-signal IC design, sensor innovation, and computational imaging for next-generation intelligent sensing technologies.

I hold a Master’s degree in Electrical Engineering (Nano- and Microelectronic Devices) from Amirkabir University of Technology (Tehran Polytechnic) and a Bachelor’s degree in Electrical Engineering (Electronics) from Iran University of Science and Technology. Previously, I conducted research in stretchable electronics, BioMEMS, and wearable biopotential monitoring systems. My goal is to integrate innovative sensor architectures with signal processing to develop powerful and versatile sensing solutions for imaging and healthcare applications.
